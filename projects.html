IMRAA Chapters

IMRAA has clubs in colleges and high schools around the world in order to implement female empowerment initiatives and grant girls the chance to lead. IMRAA is a growing network, with chapters now at our partner schools in Ghana, at Thomas A. Edison High School in Virginia, at the University of Virginia, at the University of Dayton, and beyond.

The IMRAA chapter at The College of William & Mary served as our pilot club for the 2018-2019 school year. Led by students, they hosted many "Sisters Discuss" sessions on prevalent, universal themes of females’ lives and gave a voice to a diverse range of females on campus. They held guest-speaker sessions and movie nights with relevant messages to IMRAA’s cause.

IMRAA in Ghana

IMRAA is currently partnering with activists in Ghana to connect with local high schools in order to implement chapters and initiatives, led by local girls. During our first year of partnership, we supported a craftsmanship workshop and raised funds to support 17 girls' tuition in the Northern Region of Ghana.

IMRAA recognizes that accompanying the global aspect of our organization is the need for heightened awareness to the cultural and contextual needs of each IMRAA chapter. Thus, IMRAA is highly individualized to meet the needs and desires of each community. To ensure this, our chapters are run primarily by local men and women and are heavily reliant on input from girls in the community.
